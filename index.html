Ski Service

The ideal start into your winter season.

Even though you should maintain your skis regularly, the most important ski service, is the one before the season. We evaluate the current state of your material and check what needs to be done. Waxing, grinding, surface repair if necessary and the right configuration for your ski bindings are important steps for the right start into your season

Repair

Small scrathes are repaired using a drip-fill
method.

Melting

Large scratches and damage are fixed by melting material into affected areas.

Grinding

The base, structure, and edges are precisely ground for optimal performance.

Finishing

For the perfect finish, skis and snowboards are waxed and polished to glide smoothly.

Blue Pearl von Montana

Sharp edges and gliding material.

With the high-precision machines from MONTANA your ski or board will be ground and waxed over the entire length at a precisely defined angle with the newest technology. You ski or board will run and turn much better. Sharp edges require less muscle effort from your part and increase your performance automatically.

Good To Know (FAQs)

What is a ski service?

A ski service gets your gear ready with sharp edges, a smooth base, fast wax, and the optimal binding setup.

When should you get a ski service?

A ski service is best done at the start of the season and after every 5-6 ski days. At the end of the season, it’s important to have your skis professionally prepared for storage

What does ski service include?

A ski service includes repairing base damage (e.g., from rocks or branches), grinding the base and edges for better glide and turning performance, and waxing the skis.

How long does ski service take?

If you drop off your skis by 5 PM, they’ll be ready for pick-up the next day when the lifts open..

Can skis be serviced during the day so I can ski afterwards

At our Schlossalmbahn Valley Station location, same-day service can be arranged with our team.
